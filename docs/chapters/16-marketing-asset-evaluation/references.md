[Visual identity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Visual_identity) - Wikipedia - Offers an accessible overview of Visual identity, including definitions, methods, examples, limitations, and related concepts. This foundation helps students reason carefully about evaluating visual identity, pages, campaigns, messages, calls to action, and competitive differentiation.

2. [Landing page](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Landing_page) - Wikipedia - Offers an accessible overview of Landing page, including definitions, methods, examples, limitations, and related concepts. Its examples help students evaluate evidence for evaluating visual identity, pages, campaigns, messages, calls to action, and competitive differentiation.

3. [Advertising](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Advertising) - Wikipedia - Offers an accessible overview of Advertising, including definitions, methods, examples, limitations, and related concepts. It supplies useful context for decisions about evaluating visual identity, pages, campaigns, messages, calls to action, and competitive differentiation.

4. Designing Brand Identity (6th ed.) - Alina Wheeler and Rob Meyerson - Wiley - Provides a process for brand research, strategy, identity systems, touchpoints, implementation, governance, and evaluation through case studies. Its sustained treatment supports work on evaluating visual identity, pages, campaigns, messages, calls to action, and competitive differentiation.

Don't Make Me Think, Revisited - Steve Krug - New Riders - Uses practical examples to explain clarity, navigation, scanning, conventions, task success, usability testing, and accessible digital experiences. Its cases illuminate tradeoffs involved in evaluating visual identity, pages, campaigns, messages, calls to action, and competitive differentiation.

Its comparisons clarify choices involved in evaluating visual identity, pages, campaigns, messages, calls to action, and competitive differentiation.

Its framework strengthens responsible work on evaluating visual identity, pages, campaigns, messages, calls to action, and competitive differentiation.

[Customer Journey Map: Definition and Process](https://www.interaction-design.org/literature/topics/customer-journey-map) - Interaction Design Foundation - Introduces journey-map components, scenarios, stages, touchpoints, emotions, pain points, opportunities, and the need for continuing customer research.
