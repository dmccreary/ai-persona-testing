    Cover Image Prompt¶

    Please generate a professional-quality cover image for this textbook. This image will be used in social media previews and must follow the formatting guidelines for an Open Graph image preview.

    Required specifications:

    • Format: PNG
    • Wide-landscape format
    • Size: 1200x630 pixels (1.91:1 aspect ratio)
    • This is the Open Graph standard for social media previews

    The image has four layers, back to front: background montage, color treatment, mascot, and title text.

    Subject & Tone¶

    AI Persona Testing is a practical textbook about designing no-code, AI-assisted systems that use synthetic customer personas to evaluate marketing assets while keeping evidence, uncertainty, bias, privacy, and human oversight visible. The intended audience is marketing professionals, brand and product strategists, UX and customer-experience researchers, consultants, agency teams, business leaders, and other practitioners who do not need programming experience. The visual tone should be modern, analytical, approachable, and evidence-focused: sophisticated enough for working professionals, but never futuristic for its own sake.

    Title¶

    Place the exact title AI Persona Testing in the center of the image in a clean, bold, highly legible sans-serif font. Use a light or white font color with a subtle drop shadow and a calm dark-blue scrim behind it so it remains readable against the montage. Preserve the exact spelling and capitalization. Keep the title large enough to be readable in a small social-media preview; simplify or soften the background immediately behind it rather than shrinking the title. Do not add a subtitle or any other large text.

    Background Montage¶

    Arrange a montage of the following eight concepts around the title. Render every element in one polished flat-vector technical-illustration style so the composition reads as a single designed image rather than a collage. Favor clear visual metaphors and recognizable shapes over tiny interface text.

    1. Customer persona design — a small, diverse set of abstract persona profile cards with simple goal, motivation, need, and behavior symbols; avoid photorealistic faces and demographic stereotypes.
    2. Jobs-to-be-Done and customer journey — a clean pathway moving through trigger, evaluation, decision, and outcome stages, with subtle push/pull and friction markers.
    3. Prompt architecture — modular prompt blocks for role, context, task, constraints, evidence, and structured output fitting together like a precise system; use icons rather than readable paragraphs.
    4. Multi-agent evaluation — distinct persona, moderator, skeptic, and expert nodes contributing independent findings to a bounded round-table workflow, with human review clearly positioned at the decision point.
    5. Marketing asset evaluation — simplified website, advertisement, message, and visual-identity tiles being examined through an evidence-focused lens; do not reproduce real brands or logos.
    6. Rubrics and confidence calibration — a compact evaluation matrix with anchored rating marks, evidence indicators, confidence bands, and a balanced scale rather than a decorative five-star score.
    7. Evidence triangulation and responsible AI — three independent evidence streams converging on a supported claim, paired with a small shield or guardrail motif representing bias checks, privacy, and human oversight.
    8. Knowledge graph and actionable reporting — a sparse network linking persona, asset, goal, pain point, reaction, evidence, and recommendation nodes, flowing into a clear executive dashboard and prioritized action list.

    Mascot¶

    Place Lens the Evidence Explorer, the book's mascot, in the lower-left corner, sized so they do not overlap the title. Use docs/img/mascot/welcome.png as the visual reference. Lens is an anthropomorphic magnifying glass with a circular blue-grey rim and slender arms, a pale-blue face, and a deep-orange handle forming a compact body. They have calm, curious eyes, a subtle friendly closed-mouth smile, simple four-fingered hands, and small stable feet. Both hands remain free in an open, welcoming gesture. Lens wears and carries no clothing, notebook, clipboard, pointer, or other accessory. Preserve the mascot's clean dark outlines, geometric forms, minimal shading, proportions, and established colors exactly.

    Style & Composition¶

    • Illustration style: polished modern flat-vector technical illustration with clean dark outlines, crisp geometric forms, restrained depth, and minimal shading. Apply the same style to every montage element and to Lens.
    • Color palette: blue-grey (#546E7A) and deep navy as the structural base, pale blue (#E1F5FE) for open space and evidence pathways, and deep orange (#FF5722) as a controlled accent for priority findings and Lens's body. Small teal accents may distinguish validated evidence.
    • Lighting and mood: bright, calm, precise, trustworthy, and optimistic; professional rather than playful, ominous, or science-fictional.
    • Composition: title centered; the eight montage concepts arranged as a loose ring or balanced left-to-right workflow around it; Lens in the lower-left; generous negative space and a subtle dark scrim immediately behind the title; strongest detail toward the outer thirds so the center stays clear.
    • Visual hierarchy: the title is dominant, Lens is the secondary focal point, and the montage rewards closer inspection without becoming visual noise.
    • Safe area: keep all essential title lettering and Lens comfortably inside the frame so social platforms do not crop them.

    Avoid¶

    • Do not render dense paragraphs, tiny UI instructions, or illegible pseudo-text anywhere in the image.
    • Do not misspell, restyle, duplicate, or add words to the title.
    • Avoid generic AI imagery such as glowing humanoid robots, chrome brains, circuit-board faces, neon cyberspace, or disembodied hands touching holograms.
    • Avoid generic marketing stock-photo cliches such as handshakes, isolated lightbulbs, people pointing at whiteboards, megaphones, target icons, and unexplained upward-trending arrows.
    • Avoid photorealistic human faces, demographic caricatures, and visual cues that could imply synthetic personas are substitutes for real customer evidence.
    • Do not depict Lens as an AI authority, a customer, or a source of evidence.
    • Do not give Lens accessories or change their palette, geometry, proportions, expression, or flat-vector style.
    • Do not let montage elements compete with, touch, or overlap the title or Lens.
    • Do not use multiple illustration styles, excessive gradients, busy textures, or more than a few controlled accent colors.
