    1. Which definition best describes Prompt Specificity?¶

    1. The degree to which instructions have one understandable interpretation and use direct, consistent language.
    2. Named placeholders whose values change between runs while the surrounding prompt remains stable.
    3. The amount of concrete detail that narrows a prompt’s task, audience, evidence, constraints, and expected response.
    4. Recording prompt revisions and their results so teams can reproduce, compare, and restore tested instructions.
    Show Answer

    The correct answer is C. The amount of concrete detail that narrows a prompt’s task, audience, evidence, constraints, and expected response. Recognizing the precise definition prevents the term from being substituted for a nearby but different idea. The alternatives instead point toward Prompt Clarity, Prompt Variables, Prompt Versioning, or detach the concept from the evidence and decision it is meant to support. Keeping those ideas distinct helps the team state its method, claim, and limits explicitly.
