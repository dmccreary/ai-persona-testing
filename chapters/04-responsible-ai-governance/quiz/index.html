    Quiz: Responsible AI, Privacy, and Governance¶

    Test your understanding of responsible ai, privacy, and governance with these review questions.

    1. Which definition best describes Claim Verification?¶

    1. Checking a statement against reliable evidence to determine whether it is supported, qualified, contradicted, or unresolved.
    2. A person’s voluntary agreement to participate after receiving understandable information about the research, risks, data practices, and choices.
    3. The practice of collecting, retaining, and exposing only the information necessary for a defined and legitimate purpose.
    4. Comparison of multiple methods, sources, or perspectives to determine whether a finding remains credible across independent evidence.
    Show Answer

    The correct answer is A. Checking a statement against reliable evidence to determine whether it is supported, qualified, contradicted, or unresolved. Recognizing the precise definition prevents the term from being substituted for a nearby but different idea. The alternatives instead point toward Informed Consent, Data Minimization, Evidence Triangulation, or detach the concept from the evidence and decision it is meant to support. Keeping those ideas distinct helps the team state its method, claim, and limits explicitly.
